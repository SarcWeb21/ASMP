About Us
Alumni Student Mentorship Programme (ASMP) is an initiative by Student Alumni Relation Cell with an objective of creating a platform to foster relationships between alumni mentors and students that last forever. ASMP achieves this aim by bringing together alumni mentors and student mentees through various events to help form a rapport and strengthen the connection between them, thus enhancing the IITB Alumni network.

FAQs

Approximately how many days will it take to get the mentor and further information regarding the process:
You will be allocated a mentor or be given further information regarding your allocation within 15 days.
What is the time for which I will be allocated a mentor
Your mentor is being assigned to you for a year, officially, but most people build lifelong bonds with their mentors and remain in touch even after the programme ends.
I'm having problems with the login. What should I do?
(mention general troubleshooting measures after asking the web team) If this doesn't work out, contact an ASMP coordinator as soon as possible.
How will I get notified about the process:
You will receive emails notifying you about all the updates regarding the allocation. You will also receive contact details of your allocated mentor and information about the interactive sessions scheduled by us via email.
How will my personal details be used:
Your personal details such as your name, roll number, and contact information will remain confidential till the allocation procedure. Only the allocated mentor will have access to your contact information.
What is the process after registration for a mentee:
A mentor will be allocated to you based on your preferences and SOPs after the registration is completed. You have to initiate the conversation with your mentor and then you can mutually decide the way ahead. We will also have a few follow-ups for your assistance.

  • Break The Ice

    Break The Ice kickstarts the mentoring process through a lunch-meeting for the mentor and mentee, which also fosters a personal interaction between the two.

    Mentor-Mentee Meetup

    An open house discussion between alumni and mentees aimed at evaluation of the current ASMP structure through the lens of both alumni and students.

    Shadow Programme

    The Shadow Programme lets mentees have a taste of the professional life of their mentor alumni by arranging a visit to the workplace of the mentor.

    Group Mentoring

    Mentors are invited to interact with a group of 4-5 students to together give them insight into new ideas, opportunities and career options of their interest.
